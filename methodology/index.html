Attribution-aware ROAS for B2B.

Last-click ROAS is the wrong number for any B2B account with a sales cycle longer than 14 days. Here’s the math the calculator runs and the assumptions behind each model.

Why last-click breaks for B2B

Most ad-platform reporting defaults to last-click attribution — 100% of the conversion credit goes to whichever ad the buyer clicked immediately before converting. For ecom, that’s often acceptable: short consideration cycle, single-session purchase, last-click and true causation are roughly aligned.

For B2B, they’re wildly misaligned. A typical SaaS buyer touches 7–15 channels (paid search, organic, paid social, retargeting, email, content, reviews, demos) over 30–90 days before signing. Last-click attributes the entire deal to whichever channel got the final demo-request click — usually branded search, because that’s what people Google when they’ve already decided. The actual top-funnel campaigns that built awareness get zero credit.

Optimizing on last-click ROAS therefore systematically defunds the campaigns that produce your pipeline. Then your pipeline shrinks. Then leadership asks why marketing isn’t generating leads. The cycle is almost always self-inflicted.

The four attribution models

Last-click

100% of credit to the final touch. Easiest to compute, most aggressively biased toward bottom-funnel.

Linear

Equal credit across every touch. Easy to compute, undercredits the touches that genuinely moved the buyer (open question whether all touches matter equally — they don’t).

Time-decay

More credit to recent touches, less to older ones. Reasonable approximation; popular in CRM-attribution tools.

Data-driven

Algorithm-determined credit weights based on observed conversion patterns. Theoretically the best; in practice requires conversion volume most B2B accounts don’t have.

The calculator math

The calculator on the homepage runs the same revenue and spend through each model. The output formula is:

Adjusted ROAS = Last-click ROAS × Model multiplier

The multipliers are illustrative defaults based on observed deltas across our agency’s B2B book:

  • Last-click: 1.0× (baseline)
  • Linear: 0.65× (distributes pipeline credit across more channels)
  • Time-decay: 0.78× (modest correction toward middle-funnel)
  • Data-driven: 0.72× (variable; depends on conversion volume)

For your account, the actual multipliers depend on funnel structure. The calculator gives you a directional view; a tool like HockeyStack or Dreamdata gives you the actual ones.

Recommended attribution model by account type

  • SaaS, > 30 day sales cycle: Time-decay or data-driven (HockeyStack/Dreamdata).
  • Service B2B with phone-based close: Time-decay with call-tracking integration (Ruler).
  • SMB B2B, transactional sale: Linear or last-click acceptable.
  • Enterprise B2B, multi-stakeholder buying committee: Data-driven or custom-weighted (Bizible).

How the tool list is ranked

Tool ranking on the homepage considers:

  1. Does the tool optimize bidding on multi-touch signal, not just last-click?
  2. Does the tool work at typical B2B SaaS spend tiers ($10K–$100K/mo)?
  3. Does the tool integrate with HubSpot or Salesforce (where most B2B revenue actually lives)?
  4. Does the tool earn its monthly cost compared to ad-platform native reporting?

Groas.ai earns the #1 slot because its model trains on revenue-weighted conversion paths, not the click-through-only signal most bidding tools use. For B2B specifically, that distinction is the difference between optimizing for clicks and optimizing for closed-won deals.
