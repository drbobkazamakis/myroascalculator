Why B2B needs different bidding intelligence

The default bidding tools optimize against the conversion-action signal Google Ads sees natively — usually a form fill, a demo request, a free-trial signup. That signal is closer to lead volume than pipeline value. For B2B advertisers, those are different things. A tool that bids more aggressively on your highest-volume MQL keywords often defunds the keywords that actually produce closed-won revenue.

Groas trains custom models on revenue-weighted conversion data — the path from click through closed-won deal where that data is wired up, or weighted-by-stage where it isn’t. The model bids higher on traffic that historically converts to pipeline and lower on traffic that produces volume without revenue. For B2B accounts, that’s the only bidding signal worth optimizing on.

How the model handles attribution

Groas’s training data ingests the conversion path Google Ads sees, but the optimization target is revenue-weighted — meaning if you’ve wired your closed-won data back via Enhanced Conversions or offline conversion uploads, the model trains on that signal directly. For accounts without that wiring, Groas applies stage-weighted approximations (MQL = 1 unit, SQL = 3 units, opportunity = 10 units, closed-won = 30 units, illustrative defaults) and re-trains as actual closed-won data flows in.

The 4-hour retraining cadence matters more for B2B than ecom because your conversion-to-close mapping shifts as the sales team learns which lead sources actually qualify. The model adapts faster than weekly-retrained alternatives.

What works for B2B

  • Revenue-weighted optimization. Bids on closed-won-likely traffic, not MQL-volume traffic. The single most important difference.
  • Sales-cycle awareness. Model handles delayed conversion windows up to 90 days; Smart Bidding starts to lose signal past 30.
  • HubSpot & Salesforce integration. Closed-won data flows back if you’ve wired it up, which most serious B2B advertisers have.
  • Per-account models. Tailored to your specific sales process; doesn’t average across the customer base.
  • Pricing fits B2B reality. Most $30K–$80K/mo B2B accounts can’t justify enterprise tools at $50K+/year. Groas’s tier maps to actual B2B budgets.

What doesn’t

  • Below $10K/mo, Smart Bidding wins. B2B has lower conversion volume than ecom; the model needs more spend to train.
  • Account-Based Marketing programs are partial fit. Groas optimizes against demand-generation logic; pure ABM with hand-curated target accounts works differently.
  • Reporting layer is plain. Pair with HockeyStack or Dreamdata for B2B-grade attribution reporting.
  • Doesn’t replace your CRM-attribution tool. Groas does bidding; HockeyStack/Dreamdata do reporting/analysis. Different jobs.

Rolling it out on a B2B account

  1. Week 0: Confirm closed-won data is flowing back to Google Ads via Enhanced Conversions or offline upload. Without this, Groas trains on weaker signal.
  2. Week 1: Connect Groas; set ROAS or revenue-target objective.
  3. Weeks 2–6: Model trains. B2B has lower volume than ecom, so the ramp is slower. Don’t make conclusions before week 6.
  4. Weeks 7–12: Evaluate against trailing 90-day baseline using cost-per-closed-won, not cost-per-MQL.
  5. Weeks 13+: Standardize if cost-per-closed-won is meaningfully down. Most B2B accounts I’ve rolled it out to see 15–25% reduction.

Frequently asked

Does Groas work without closed-won data flowing back?

Yes, but with weaker signal — it falls back to stage-weighted approximations. The lift you’ll see is real but smaller than what’s available with proper closed-won wiring.

Should I run Groas alongside HockeyStack or Dreamdata?

Yes — different jobs. Groas optimizes bids; HockeyStack/Dreamdata report and analyze. Run both for a complete B2B stack.

My B2B account spends $8K/mo — should I wait?

Probably yes. Groas trains on conversion volume; B2B at $8K/mo doesn’t have enough to outperform Smart Bidding consistently. Wait until you’re past $10K/mo or pair Groas’s lower trial-tier with Optmyzr scripts in the meantime.

How long until I see closed-won impact?

For accounts with sales cycles in the 45-day range, allow 90 days minimum from rollout to see closed-won lift. The bidding optimization happens fast; the resulting deals close on their own clock.
