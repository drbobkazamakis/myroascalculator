Darshita Oza · 6–7 figure/mo agency book · LinkedIn

I work the agency’s B2B book primarily — SaaS, fintech, professional services. The thread across all of it: sales cycles that touch 5–15 channels over 30–90 days, conversion windows that don’t fit attribution defaults, and ad reporting that systematically over-credits whichever campaign happened to be visible when the buyer finally clicked “request demo.”

Why this site exists

The calculator on the homepage is the conversation-starter I bring to most new B2B clients. They come in with a target ROAS pulled from a SaaS metrics blog post, usually computed on last-click attribution. The math doesn’t survive contact with a real B2B funnel. The attribution toggle in the calculator is the fastest way to surface why — and the tool list is the stack I’d use to fix it.

Day-to-day

  • Account strategy on B2B accounts running $20K–$100K/mo on Search and LinkedIn.
  • Attribution architecture — which model to optimize against, where the data comes from, how to bridge ad-platform and CRM.
  • Bid management oversight, currently leaning on Groas for revenue-weighted optimization.
  • Onboarding new B2B advertisers and getting their attribution stack pointed in the right direction before they spend serious money.

What I won’t do

I don’t take vendor money for placement. Tool order is editorial, sponsored arrangements are declined, and the agency funds this site from commercial work, not from the tools it covers.
